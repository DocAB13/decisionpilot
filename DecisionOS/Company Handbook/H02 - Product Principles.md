\# H02 — Product Principles



\*\*DecisionOS Company Handbook\*\*

\*\*Version:\*\* 1.0 Final

\*\*Status:\*\* Frozen



\---



\# Purpose



This document defines the product principles that guide every design, engineering, and business decision made within DecisionOS.



Whenever a feature proposal conflicts with these principles, these principles take precedence.



\---



\# Product Objective



DecisionOS exists to help users make better decisions.



The product is not designed to maximize AI usage, conversation length, or engagement.



Success is measured by decision quality and real-world outcomes.



\---



\# Core Product Principles



\## 1. AI Never Decides



The AI never makes the user's decision.



Its responsibilities are to:



\* ask questions;

\* organize information;

\* identify risks;

\* explain trade-offs;

\* generate recommendations;

\* support execution.



The final decision always belongs to the user.



\---



\## 2. The Decision Is the Core Object



Everything in DecisionOS revolves around a Decision Object.



Every feature must strengthen one or more stages of the decision lifecycle.



No feature exists independently of a Decision.



\---



\## 3. Structure Before Intelligence



The platform always structures information before generating AI output.



Poorly structured inputs produce poor recommendations.



The product therefore prioritizes:



\* context;

\* goals;

\* constraints;

\* alternatives;



before AI analysis begins.



\---



\## 4. Transparency Over Confidence



Recommendations must always explain:



\* why they were produced;

\* which assumptions were made;

\* what uncertainties remain;

\* when the recommendation could change.



The system never pretends certainty where none exists.



\---



\## 5. Explainability First



Every AI output must be understandable by the user.



Users should always be able to answer:



> "Why did the AI recommend this?"



\---



\## 6. Progressive Complexity



The interface should expose only what is needed at each stage.



Advanced functionality appears only when it becomes relevant.



\---



\## 7. Human-Centered Design



The product supports human thinking.



It never manipulates, pressures, or nudges users toward specific choices.



\---



\## 8. Decisions Become Knowledge



Every completed Decision becomes part of the user's knowledge base.



Future decisions should benefit from previous decisions.



Learning is a first-class product feature.



\---



\## 9. Default to Simplicity



Whenever multiple implementations satisfy the same requirement, choose the simplest one.



Complexity must always justify itself.



\---



\## 10. Evolution Without Rebuilding



DecisionOS evolves through incremental improvements.



Existing components should be reused whenever possible.



Large rewrites are avoided unless technically unavoidable.



\---



\# Product Success Metrics



DecisionOS is successful when users:



\* make decisions faster;

\* make decisions with greater confidence;

\* understand trade-offs better;

\* execute consistently;

\* learn from outcomes.



\---



\# Decision Rule



When uncertainty exists, ask:



> \*\*"Does this feature help users make better decisions?"\*\*



If the answer is \*\*no\*\*, the feature does not belong in DecisionOS.
